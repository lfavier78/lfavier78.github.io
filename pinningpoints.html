Dynamic influence of pinning points on marine ice-sheet stability

Paper accepted for publication in The Cryosphere link

The stability of the East Antarctic ice sheet is less debated than its west Antarctic counterpart. In this modelling study, we use the ice-sheet model BISICLES to explore the impact of various sub-ice shelf melting scenarios on the stability of Dronning Maud Land, East Antarctica. Here below we show the response of the Hansen glacier over the next millenium for the intermediate melting scenario, which reproduces best the observed melting, both in amplitude and distribution.



The retreat of the ice sheet is obviously highly driven by the sub-ice shelf melting scenarios. However, more importantly, we also show that the use of non accurate datasets (velocity and ice geometry) for initialising an ice sheet prior to transient simulations, can spuriously impact the grounding-line retreat and hence increase the uncertainties in future sea level predictions.
